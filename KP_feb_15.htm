JRS

A very pleasant day.  Got up late, had a leisurely breakfast, walked down Deer gorge and around down to the falls and fished. It was wonderful fishing.  I caught one in the first cast using salmon eggs, and another soon after. Then, Tommy caught one and I got my third.  Tommy evened it up and we had six fish in about 2 hours. Very enjoyable fishing. They ranged from 8 to 10in. and were both male and female. The eggs were all gone so they must have already spawned, though the males still have some milt in them. We sautéed them in butter, and of course they were superb. Then we came back up here to the valley and rested some more. Ate fish.  

Explored the ruins (very faint traces and small). There appeared to be a sort of stone wall built into the hillside above them that extended a 100ft. or so.  We didn’t know what to make of that. Went through the canebrake in the valley. 12ft. high! About 3acres of near impenetrable jungle. We did find a trace though and threaded our way through them to climb up the east side to Deer Spring. This is a pretty good size source issuing from a crack about halfway down the Temple Butte formation.

An enjoyable day though it was gray until this evening when a few patches of cloud caught pink fire from the setting sun in a break to the west out of sight of us.  And now a few stars are showing through. We dare not let ourselves be given false hopes for fear of disappointment, yet my heart skips a beat!  In view of tomorrow’s no doubt difficult ascent up to the cache, I’ve been doing some fretting. To put my incipient paranoia to some practical use I’ve been conjuring up some of the more gruesome possible scenarios in order to anticipate and be prepared for them.  What it does come down to in the end is not much.  Just keep the victim warm and wait.  Still that can be a great deal, and we’re both of us vigorous people. Actually the very best thing we can do is to make no mistakes. Accidents are caused they don’t just happen. To cultivate a healthy paranoia and balance that with a cheerful optimism based on knowledge and personal confidence is the state of mind I seek and I believe will help get me through this.

More, much more yet to come.

 TWS

 Rest day. We’re still a day ahead of schedule -- the permit reads “camped at Deer Creek 2/15”. We camped here last night, and we’ll be here again tonight and tomorrow night. Tomorrow early we set out up the Deer Creek drainage to retrieve the cache.  Jim says he put it near the base of the Coconino. Its seven miles one way from here. We’ve debated and looked it over from several standpoints. First we’ve carried plenty of food with us and extra for safety, so right now we still have adequate food to make it to the Bass cache.

If all goes well, up and over Mauve Saddle. There’s snow up there now – we know for sure. Powell Plateau, visible from the river yesterday, was socked in with cold looking snow covering the slopes beneath the clouds. So without the cache padding our food supply it makes it a little risky to shoot for Mauve knowing we could be socked in up there and have to spend some time. The factor that decides it for us is our belief that if we stay well fed (including all the little goodies Jim put up there) we will be able to meet the worst conditions, i.e., 2ft. or more snow,  climbing up the saddle.

Today we rested, gathered our resources and harbored our energies. As we came out over Deer Creek falls on the Tapeats ledge we spotted a Great Blue Heron pick up from the water where Deer Creek enters the Colorado. He flapped across the river and landed on a great boulder of Vishnu.  He had sensed our movement on the ledge. Then he gave up his fishing spot to us amateurs, and flew off upstream.

With the aid of hooks and line and salmon eggs brought along we were successful where the Heron uses only his great patience and speed.

We cooked up the trout this afternoon in butter and ate them by themselves. It was rich and good.  We could almost feel the energy surge from such a fine meal. This evening we had lentils with garlic, onions, cheese on tortillas. Most filling and it leaves me feeling we have fueled our bodies well for the day’s work ahead.
