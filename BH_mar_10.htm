           TWS

             I write this at Hualapai Hilltop. We have made it!  It rained last night, but we stayed dry in the tent. I woke up in the morning feverishly hot and dizzy, and was afraid I was coming down with something.  Was still light headed when we took off this morning, but I’m feeling great now. We left around nine or ten in the morning and got here around 1:30 or so. The trail up the canyon is long and winding through the Supai, taking most of the ten miles to rise up over the layer (finally off the Esplanade!), and up through the Coconino now to the picnic table where we sit.

 The women will be here any minute in the V. W. to move us back through space and time, back to Griffin Tank and Albuquerque. All things move in spirals. We complete our physical movement through this trip with that last steep climb up the switchbacks on the Coconino. Our last spiritual gesture to the Canyon is our paho –owl feather and raven feather -- these mark our path on this journey at Bass, Spencer, Great Thumb, and now at Hualapai Hilltop.
