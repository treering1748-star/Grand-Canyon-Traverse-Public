TWS

Stars shone all last night. Each time I awoke and peered out from under the fly I could see them. When morning light finally illuminated the cliffs of King Arthur’s Castle, skies were gray all around. Our hopes for more sun were shattered, and my spirit turned bitter, resigned. Tis our fate to toil in wet and cold! My attitude reflected my doubts, but 30 minutes later blue skies showed and the clouds parted.

We found a route down into Shinumo just below the junction of Merlin and Modred, and back up to the east side.  A couple of long boulder jumps across tomato red Shinumo -- high water. Several old sections of burro trails, seemingly abandoned, gave us assistance in steep and questionable spots. Same old burro story. Up here, down there --some critters likes to go high, some low. Generally, at critical route finding spots, burro trails are apt to peter out, branch off, and disappear. And then again, they’re just as likely to keep going and get you around most efficiently. The trouble is you just don’t know what they’re gonna do.

As we wound around the Tonto into Flint the sun broke out from isolated clouds and we had warmth, even when we stopped to bend down over a trickle for a sip of water. We arrived here on Flint just opposite of Gawain, stumbling because our eyes were glued to the Redwall route and not the trail. Our eyes have since searched the fault ravine in as close a detail as possible from this angle and distance. It looks “adventurous” indeed. Several steps, ramps and ledges. A long climb from here.  The Temple Butte is thick and BA shale and Mauve beneath form more than half the elevation – about 500ft. Then the Redwall adds another 400 feet.  900ft. total for just the section to the top of the Redwall. The saddle lies a couple of miles SE from up there, in the Supai. We can’t see that section tonight, but we can see the S facing Supai section opposite our route.  It’ll be brushy and steep with climbs up over Supai ledges. Anticipation.

This afternoon was spent most luxuriously – bathing, sunning, studying our route, enjoying the magnificent views. Once more, we are in a most amazing camping spot. Tapeats below our ledge carries clear Flint Creek water.  Blue sky and clouds over rock. Snow is visible up on the North Rim.

JRS

Flint Creek.  Facing the Flint route tonight. It doesn’t look like an easy route at all. From back at the junction with Shinumo you could see a system of ledges and ramps that continued up through most of the Redwall, then a difficult looking step at the top. Our angle has changed now and most of the ramp is obscured by an intervening col.  It has been difficult to study because it has been shadowed all day, and details are suppressed. It looks to be a very challenging day tomorrow, and I find myself looking forward to it. The weather was beautiful again today, very bright and warm. We bathed and rested.

Very complex geology here in Flint. The Mauve Fault has scrambled the layers here in an interesting pattern.  Shinumo quartzite on the west side of the canyon, and Tapeats sandstone on the other. It has been wonderful here in the Shinumo Amphitheater.  Here we are, on the other side of Sagittarius Ridge. I have not so much as been able to look down here before. It is totally a new canyon for me. Tomorrow we should finally be into the familiar canyon.
