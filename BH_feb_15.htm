          TWS

           The preparations are nearly complete.  I have enough food for least three weeks; enough that is to maintain my energy level.  The list is roughly as follows: 3lbs. rice enriched, 3lbs. rolled oats, 3lbs. granola, 2lbs. sugar brown and white, 1lb. instant coffee, 3lbs. trail mix - nuts and raisins – 16 soup mixes -may need more, 1lb. dried apples, 1lb. dried apricots, 1lb. tang, one stick margarine.  This food will be broken up into a one week supply a two weeks supply.  The 2 week supply will be stashed an Apache Point, that is if we if we can get out there.

Doug Logan has lent us his climbing rope - 100ft., 6lbs. and we’ve got Suzanne’s tent should we need it.  We may decide to leave it behind and only take the fly.  Woody Peterson has lent me his small 135mm auto camera since it will be more reliable than mine. 2 pair of long johns should be enough.  I’ll leave one pair at Apache.  4 pair of socks should also be enough.  I’m taking along enough canteen spare to carry two gallons.  Water shouldn’t be much of a problem with all the moisture lately, but we’ve marked all potholes and springs, that we can dig out of the literature, on the map.  Also taking along Butchart’s book and maybe Fletcher’s. 

The big problem now is logistics.  How to set the cache at Apache, and how to get out to the trailhead when the roads are so bad?  Jim called Monday night and he thinks a four wheel is necessary.  I’ve postponed going over to the Canyon till Friday in hopes things will improve.  I’m kinda regretting waiting now since things haven’t really improved.  If we can just get below the rim at Bass I think everything will be all right.  We thought about all the hardships we’re going to face in the Canyon and I feel prepared and willing to face them.  This problem with the roads, however, seems perhaps larger than it is.  I haven’t seen them yet of course but it seems that they should improve going out because of less traffic farther away from Grand Canyon Village.  Also, there’s another road out to Bass which follows the Park-Forest boundary fence - it may be in better shape.  It’s just a little frustrating sitting here and not being able to do anything about it.

When Jim called he was a little less than optimistic, but still reassuring that he still wants to make the original trip.  I think his energy is just at a low point because he’s just getting over the flu and he’s had to fight those roads between the cabin and the Village.  Cold weather also has the effect of damping any will get out in it.  I’m pretty sure at this point that at least part of our journey will be in foul weather.  No doubt there will be cold nights and mornings, but we’ll adjust.  The physical aches and pains will initially occupy our minds, but with time I know they fade.  When I think about it, our so called hardships were common place to thousands who explored and traveled the west.  In fact, they were probably much more difficult.  Consider that we have lightweight aluminum and nylon backpacks, and dried and instant foods, down clothing and sleeping bags, and dozens of other handy, efficient devices.  Compare this with frameless, (shoulder bruising) packs, hardtack and beans and coffee, and a wool blanket, and sometimes much less,  that old-timers made do on for months at a time.  This is a perspective we should keep in mind.  We cannot expect to be physically comfortable on a trip of this nature, and that isn’t the purpose of the trip anyway.

There are many bugs yet to be worked out in the plans, including the logistics and timing.  I should be back before the 2nd week of March.  But things will work out.  I remain flexible.  My mind is set on going around Great Thumb, but if it’s not feasible, there are other trips we can make.  It remains to be seen.
