TWS

 Jim’s hunch proved correct and we located the trail heading up to Surprise Valley just above Deer Falls.  Two miles of grunt work and we reached the trail ascending through the Redwall up to Indian Hollow.  We unloaded all our gear, set up the tarp and headed up. 

On top we came into Esplanade country.  Open red sandstone forming terraces above the arms of Deer Creek. Very reminiscent of the Great Thumb trip.  Long avenues of paved level sandstone made for easy walking and we stretched out our legs for speed.  Our worry was that darkness with catch us on the return trip and not being able to see the cairns laying out the route on the trackless sandstone we’d have to bivouac. 

The 11 miles up to the cache were long.   But once there we dove into a can of corn beef and dried apricots and took a rest.  Jim’s cache turned out to be everything he said it was.  Plenty for a party of four to five for up to two weeks.  Lots of goodies too.

On the return trip we both felt good and we continued walking without stop to where we had placed our water containers under a dripping overhang to fill.  Two more miles walking and home sweet home: our tarp set up on the hill above Surprise Valley.  In all -- 24 miles today. 

JRS

We found the route to Surprise Valley up the canyon above Deer Spring.  Once we got here we barely paused to rest, but set up the tarp, and set out again, up through the Redwall and across the Esplanade.  Eleven miles without a break.  We hit our stride and stretched out our legs.  It was very good walking on the Esplanade, hard level stretches, but tedious weaving around the heads of canyons.  The trail seemed unnecessarily long, no doubt because it was originally laid out for horses. 

We ate a canned lunch at the cache and rested for an hour, then poured the coals to it again all the way back here in one sustained effort.  Actually it felt pretty good.  We had psyched ourselves up for it well and our bodies are in pretty good shape now. 

My left knee started bothering me yesterday.  Too much boulder hopping, and I wrapped it with an ace bandage this morning.  I was easy on it and I soon walked the stiffness out.  It gave me no trouble, thank god. 

I would’ve liked to tarry on the Esplanade.  I love that country.  I could wander through those stone terraces and avenues and easily lose myself to them.  Spotted some Indian campsites and it would’ve been nice to browse around them a little more, but no time, no time. 

We got back here just in time to see the sunset through a break in the clouds.  It’s been doing that to us the past few days, giving us a few minor sunbeams and patches of blue just at days end, then closing up again and raining on us all night.  It can be dispiriting.  We want blue sky so badly.  Meanwhile the snow keeps piling up in Mauve Saddle. 

We’re going to take about half of the food cache with us and leave one full 5 gallon can here.  Deb and Chamisa will get their trip here on that food.  Everything came through the past 3 months as though it were only a day and what we’re leaving behind is mostly canned and dry food so it should last through next summer all right.  Will try to be here in the fall.
