          JRS

          Hard day today. Long twisted miles of boulder hopping, a problem to solve with eye, muscle and bone. Around, over or across, sometimes a good decision, sometimes a bad one. A workout of the bones and joints with every step a possible broken ankle.  Long miles but good ones too. We’re in the Redwall gorge of the Rio Colorado!  What a splendid canyon we have come down. What a grand canyon we are in.  

          High clouds this morning thickening by noon, overcast and light scattered rain this evening. Clouds are from the southwest. Have seen no big fish in Kanab. The river is running brown. Now as we lay under our tarp the wind has found us and billows to fly on its stays.

I crotched my pants today, a muffled pop, as I hoped a boulder. I had a pre-sentiment that would happen.  I stitched them up before dinner. Made myself a pair of suspenders from nylon wedding.  A helicopter passed through the gorge just before last light. A dark colored Hughes 500. The Redwall is incredible.  Just across from the Kanab confluence and about 500ft. up is an enormous cave/amphitheater with a polished chute above and below it. We passed spires and pinnacles.

            TWS

            Last night was warm – relatively speaking -- and both Jim and I slept cozy at the canyon junction. Previous nights up Kanab and Hack we were awake by the early hours of morning -- not cold but uncomfortable. Our bags are not the heaviest and probably designed for warmer temps, but we make do. Long underwear and sweaters and jackets to bed.

Now we are camped on the Colorado and Kanab junction. The air is warmer here and I expect we’ll sleep well.  It was another long day.  Winding, twisting canyon -- up over boulders down the other side, leaping across rocks over the growing stream. Mud and water saturated sand would give way to our boots and we’d lurch forward sucking them out, bringing out an extra 2lbs. of weight on the boot.

Following Jim most of the way I am amazed now and then how quickly he climbs over our obstacles and jumps to the ground on the other side, already sizing up the next boulder solution. After most of the day’s walking and the jolting rock hoping, my joints are sore.

As we came down canyon we finally cut below the Redwall formation and began the Tonto and Temple Butte series. The walls took on enormous heights, until now at the river the Redwall tops out over a thousand feet above us. Once again we are surrounded by dark crags against the sky. El Rio Colorado roars over Kanab rocks.
