JRS

Upper Shinumo. We are camped on a small rock island in the Tapeats, a slender talus bridge joins us to the side of the canyon, here 1/4 mile beneath the junction of Merlin and Modred Abysses. There are 2 crude rock shelters or ruins here, one of which we have thrown the tarp over. The sky is so clear now it may not be necessary.

 Oh, Canyon!  Such extremes! This morning the sun rose bright over Mauve Saddle, and energized by the warmth and brightness we have made a good 15 miles today.  Quite a contrast with what we encountered on the other side, and quite appropriate too, since this is entirely new country, the middle canyon, completely separated, visually from the west by Powell Plateau and Great Thumb.

The trip has entered a new phase now. The North Bass trail is certainly a bushwhacker, though we had little trouble with it, really, in comparison with the west side. I can see how going up it would be discouraging. The long traverse over the Redwall is a little tedious.  But the Redwall descent is magnificent. Again, the route traverses at or near the fault line. There is a striking fault surface in the Redwall at this point.

Many waterfalls over the Redwall. All the side canyons are flowing and main streams, Shinumo and White Creek are near torrents and red.  With this warm weather the snows on top, wet as they are, are melting fast.

Saw a burro today near Redwall canyon. He was wearing a collar. Not as many burro trails as Harvey intimated, though. The moon is near half tonight and waxing. We have good moonlight now and stars again!

 TWS

Today weather and country change. We awoke to find clear skies and hard crusted snow outside Mauve Cabin. The Cabin was good to us.  It gave us warmth and shelter, and brought back our energies from the low of our climb up here on the 20th. That was such a hard day, and so close to disaster.

Today, however, the sun was out and when we stopped to eat lunch we didn’t have to rush and get our jackets and sweaters on.  The sun warmed us and lulled us into a short post-lunch snooze. Half moon tonight with stars, and sparse clouds passing quickly.

White Creek was full with red water coming into it from all sides -- waterfalls everywhere. The upper end is boxed by the Redwall and Mauve formations, but we came out suddenly into the openness of the Tonto.  Views down Shinumo and up across to Bass Canyon and Mount Huethawali. Holy Grail and Bass Tomb loom above our campsite here on Shinumo Creek.

We have found a flat top isolated block of Tapeats with a narrow path, steep on both sides, the only approach. A good spot. Many miles covered today. We prepare for the next saddle -- Flint.
