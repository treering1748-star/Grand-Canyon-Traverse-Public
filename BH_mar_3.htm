              JRS

              The weather started breaking today. Skies partially cloudy with large patches of blue and periods of sunshine all day. We got a very late start near noon, feeling in no hurry, and then walked flat out for about three hours, arriving here, on Gatagama Terrace, above and close to the fault ravine through Olo Canyon. We made this an easy day, and tomorrow will perhaps be even easier if the sun is out and we can bask and bathe.

 We are deep in the Supai country, sandstone space place. Tomorrow, a world of rock and sun and water. Tinajas pock the terrace rocks and catch the setting sun blinding me with a thousand flashes. Rain veils sweep down from stately clouds, huge, but individuals now in the breaking storm. Slowly they pass, ponderous.  The stars shine through tonight with hard pointed brilliance through fresh, water-cleansed air.

 High, high, we’re so high.  The beauty never ends, never even really peaks it is so inexhaustible. My head reels.

 A rainbow appeared at sunset over Gatagama point.

 Tomorrow we rest.  I feel certain it will be sunny.  Sunshine, warm and dry again at last!

 After that, our route is under discussion. We are considering a walk through the fault ravine, bypassing most of Olo Canyon, testing the feasibility of following it across Matkatamiba. It looks rough, though, and steep, the only advantage being that it is much shorter than those long tedious miles that a traverse of canyons entails.  We shall see.

 Mount Sinyala is visible on our western horizon, tall and dignified. The canyon really opens up to the west of us, and the Esplanade seems like a wide valley. Our panorama has completely changed. This is the domain of the Esplanade, of the Supai sandstone. It’s a wonderful wilderness.

              TWS

              It took us only 3 hours or so to get here -- Gatagama Terrace from Tahuta.  We lucked out in finding a good burro trail and clipping off some miles in a hurry. We got off late today, taking our time and enjoying the sun. Partly cloudy today with the sun showing most of the morning, and short intermittent showers this afternoon, sometimes raining in the full sunlight.

 When we came around the first bend of the long rounded Gatagama Point we knew where our campsite would be -- on the terrace. Gatagama Terrace is capped with the red Supai sandstone. Beautifully rounded lines and carved rock. Balancing rocks everywhere, the whole terrace dotted with glittering potholes. We dropped down onto it, and immediately the surroundings took on the surreal. Broad open malls and walkways, benches and overhangs, and tumbled, rounded boulders. Walking is like on paved surface with wonderful irregularities, holes and niches. Most of the holes are brimming with water.

 We are under an overhang again tonight, a couple of hundred yards from the point of the Terrace.  The view south looks a hundred miles or so down the Colorado, broad and open at this end, but still guarded by massive cliffs on either side. Mt. Sinyala looks close enough to touch. Actually it’s about ten air miles distant, but several days walk from here due to the great distances around Olo Canyon and the giant one, Matkatamiba.

  From the point of this terrace we can look down into a small side canyon which connects with the Sinyala fault ravine. The fault cuts across perpendicular to several large canyons in the area and provides access down to the Redwall in Olo, Matkatamiba and Sinyala Canyons. We’re looking at the possibility of going down it to cut across Olo, thereby saving the many miles required to go around the head of Olo. It looks like a steep climb on the other side coming out though, and we may decide to go around.

 Jim and I are feeling very high. The Thumb is behind us. The weather is improving and we are in fine country.

 Today while searching around the mescal pit and in front of the overhang at Tahuta, I found a round piece of pottery with a hole in the center. And while walking up to the point of this terrace, I found the base of an arrowhead.
