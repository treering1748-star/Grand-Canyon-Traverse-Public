           TWS

           Another hard day!  I write now by candlelight, in the tent, twenty yards from the Canyon rim at Bass Camp. Today we walked 7 1/2 miles, but it felt like 20. We made our way from the last camp to Turquoise Canyon, then cross country to the road connecting Signal Hill with Bass. We arrived here maybe an hour after the sun’s zenith. We were very tired then, but we still planned to make it down to the Esplanade after lunch.

Then I discovered that, of all things, I was missing my sweater!  Jim and I were both beginning to wonder when things were going to go right. On top of other things, we both were getting concerned that perhaps we hadn’t brought along enough water containers. The Esplanade from here shows dry sections (long ones!) where they are exposed to the sun -- but there is snow in the protected side canyons.

I decided that I should make an effort to find my sweater since it was handmade by Suzanne, and it meant a great deal to me. After retracing my steps about three miles I decided to turn back, the sun was setting, and my energies were at minimum. Every step I took back I realized I would have to travel again back to Bass. About two miles from Turquoise I turned around and said goodbye to my sweater for good. It hurts!  But worse things could happen.

Around the fire tonight Jim and I discussed our bad luck and we have resolved to place ourselves closer in touch with the realities and dangers of this trip. We plan on a rest day tomorrow after hiking down to Spencer Terrace. Hopefully we can find the power and energy to avoid more bad luck. Certainly more caution, planning and meditation will help.

As I write I can feel the tiredness and aching in my muscles and bones, yet I have a positive and optimistic viewpoint. I’m sure that this trip will require more of my reserves before it is through, but I know I’ll come away with extra.

JRS

Trying day today. The going was very hard; much cross country work through snowdrifts and sagebrush. No mud at least. A very long 7-8 miles. Got our first view of the canyon today, looking down Turquoise Canyon. From there we lost the jeep trail to Signal Hill and began bushwhacking the long stretch. I thought we’d never make it. From the Signal Hill road to Bass Camp it was easier, and we discovered the remains of a shack and cistern about 1/2 way.  One thing about starting a trip this way is that you certainly see a lot of country you never would have otherwise.

We made it here to Bass Camp, but have gotten no further. Many bad, miasmic apprehensions have surfaced.  Have we enough water containers? Will it be too dry? Have we offended the Canyon spirits? We managed to find one leaky old plastic milk bottle with no lid that may do a pinch, but while attaching it to Tom’s pack discovered he had lost the sweater Suzanne had made him. Very, very sad. He, exhausted though he was, backtracked 6 miles, but could not find it. We’ve been doing something wrong.

In all, I think it was well that we were forced to camp here tonight, still above the rim. It has made us to reconsider our goal and the dangers involved. We are more in tune with the realities, and hopefully more on our toes. The whole undertaking now appears much larger than it did it first. I am chastened and humbled, no longer so overconfident. Yet, I think we will make it.

Tomorrow we shall rise early and greet the sun. We shall humble ourselves by the sight of the canyon. We shall make a paho and symbolically place it and ourselves at the mercy of the Grand Canyon spirits.

Jim's daily notes.
