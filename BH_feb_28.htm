           JRS

           Dripping, soggy, wet day. We have not left Fossil Canyon. The relative dryness of our rock shoulder was just too tempting to leave this morning, and Tom is having difficulties with a sore leg and foot.  Hopefully he’ll be able to continue OK, but the thought of him in pain makes me worrisome. We are pretty uncomfortable at the moment; mud everywhere and water dripping down from the rock every point. Still, this is in a way what I had been hoping for. Now every pot hole in the canyon is probably overflowing. So, though we are uncomfortable and hiking will be extended misery tomorrow, in the long run this has greatly increased our chances of making it intact. We are prepared for this type of situation and lord knows I’ve camped wet before.

Tried to walk down canyon to the Redwall today, but it was very rough and drizzly all the way, and the idea lost its appeal after about a mile. Spent the rest of the day cooped up under this rock trying to read Nietzche and “Geology of the Grand Canyon”. I’m about ready to use both books as fire starters. Severe case of cabin fever.

We have to gather wood constantly for the fire. It’s all bar berry, and soaking wet. Takes forever to dry, then burns to ashes in minutes. While gathering this afternoon, I discovered what looks like 3 or 4 mescal pits. These seem to be ubiquitous in this country.

So despite our wet spirits, clothes, and sleeping bags we are after all in an area of considerable interest.  Understatement. We elected to meet these difficulties, so we really have no room for self pity. This is the path, the marrow of life, and these are its terms. Has it ever been any different?

Jim's daily notes.

              TWS

               It was still raining this morning when we woke up, and from our sleeping bags we unanimously decided to make this a rest day. The fact that my left leg is very sore also played in our decision. I’m not sure if it’s just muscle stiffness or tendons, or what or how it happened, it’s just a little tender. The blister on my left foot is also causing some problems. Hopefully things will improve by tomorrow as we will be heading out for the tip of the Thumb, rain or shine.

 We spent most of the day here under our overhanging-shelter trying to amuse ourselves, but still getting pretty bored. Meanwhile it came down hard for a while then softly, then hard again all day. I have to keep reminding myself this is desert country, even though it is dripping wet.

 We made an aborted effort at about midday to walk down canyon to see how far we could get. We went about a half mile and started getting rough-boulders and drop offs, and I decided my leg should be recuperating instead -- it was hurting. It has gotten better since this morning and I believe I can walk on it OK, and the blisters will be a nuisance, but tolerable. We are hoping it’ll clear up tomorrow, as the thought of walking in the rain all day has no appeal for us. But if we have to, we have to.

 We’ve been hearing an owl, or a pair of them, hooting all day, just a ways up the canyon. As soon as the rain settles a little, they start up.

 Jim discovered directly behind our shelter here a couple of large mescal pits and two small ones.

 In general, our morale is good but we have periods of self pity and doubt. It is an extremely difficult thing we are doing and there are many hardships involved. Most of the time, however, we retain that perspective that we are in the most beautiful and unique place and we must meet this country on its own terms.  That is the decision we made by coming here, and we cheerfully accept its consequences.
