JRS

Bright Angel campground. We have made it. Though we are still within the Canyon’s depths, we are undoubtedly back in civilization.  As I write this I am slightly drunk (4 beers, full Phantom Ranch price) and already deeply culture shocked.

You tell people you’ve been out for 18 days, and you tell them where you’ve been, and there is a sort of blank politeness. They don’t really comprehend, it means nothing to them. What are we to expect any way? Brass bands and ticker tape?  Not exactly, but I would like to brag a little.  That will come of course, when we are finally among friends, among our people. They know. So I tell myself, and yet I know the only person who it will ever really mean anything to is myself. And Tommy. And yes I think, Deb. Oh Deb, I long to share it with you, more than anyone. Someday, my heart, perhaps we can do it together.

We managed to talk to the ladies this evening through Ben Morgan’s kind assistance in letting us use the ranger phone (strictly for emergencies). Quite contrary to my expectations there was very little bad news. Oh thank you gods for letting me skate for so long. Unable to contact Larry, busy signal, will try in the morning. We are trying to get the VW waiting for us at Yaki, so we can exit via the Kiabab. 

Long day hiking today. Over the Shiva Saddle. Gorgeous weather. Beautiful vistas everywhere, perfect conditions. Rappelled once today off the Supai cliffs and again through the Redwall, though we didn’t strictly need to. It was fun and made good pictures. Used the rope finally for something besides hauling packs. Walked out of Phantom via the high trail below Cheops. Am not sure we picked the easiest route as it was hard work. Spectacular trail though and one I’ve wanted to do for a while. Coming across Utah flats was quick and easy, though it’s the kind of country that invites you to stay awhile.

At Phantom we gave em a sob story, and they opened the snack bar for us. Ham sandwiches, lemonade and candy bars. $10.00. Thank god they take Visa cards. But no employee bennies. I don’t know the right people.  Larry and Beth were just down here yesterday. They might have been a meal ticket, but we missed them. No trail crew down here either. In some ways I feel like an orphan child. We hike out tomorrow though. There has been much, very much about this hike that will take me a long time to assimilate. It’s been a large walk.

TWS

Civilization. Bright Angel campground, people, beer, candy bars; all these things shock us back into their reality. Tomorrow, up the trail to the rim.

It was a long walk today. A steady climb up a Dragon side canyon, then up a boulder ramp and we were up Shiva. Then came the hard part -- getting down. The Supai on the Phantom side has almost un-climbable benches, and we finally hauled out the rope and carabiners for a rappel.

Jim’s route through the Redwall proved to be very interesting. A climb down a gully to a long narrow ledge, winding around Redwall corners and down. A short walk along the creek bed then up to the Tonto for one last scenic route.

Sandwich and lemonade, outrageously priced at Phantom, revived our energies. A few beers later, then a call to the ladies. Ah, how sweet to hear her voice. All’s well.

[We hiked out on the 28th, up the South Kiabab Trail to Yaki Point.  There is no journal entry for that day.  My recollection is that the hike up was uneventful.  We were super fit by this point, and made it out by mid-day. I do remember an ice cream cone at the top! I don't remember how we retrieved the VW from Larry, but of course we did, and drove on home to the Mimbres.  TWS, March, 2005]
