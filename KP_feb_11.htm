TWS 

Jumped up out of the sack with the first peak of the sun over a Kanab rim five miles distant -- this morning.   This afternoon I looked back up Hack canyon from that rim.  Jim and I reached this creek bottom before noon, took a look around and decided this was a beautiful spot and the day was bright and warm and this was the place to camp.

A climb up to the east side out of Kanab and to the south of Lawson brought us out on Esplanade sandstone.  Space country.  Up Kanab.  Up Hack.  Up Lawson. In all directions, rock!  Balancing, bisected corridors of boulders.  Not a cloud in the sky, just blue.

Wandered around up there over the sandstone pavement under and around the boulders and sculptures.  The eye picked out some rock out of place and we discovered a line of Cairns, footprints (old old ) hoof prints (old) headed SE.  Jim thinks toward Jump Up.  Following the cairns back down into Kanab it developed into a trail -- somewhat.

Back down on the Kanab and baths.  Cold!  Cold!  Using a big cooking pot we doused ourselves, again and again with the ice water.  The walls echoed with our samurai yells.  Much, much better afterwards.  We dried in the sun -- a February sun, but quite warm.

A walk this evening up Kanab brought us beneath an overhanging cliff with pictographs.  Figures of men, big horn and a red corkscrew design.  Pot shards and flakes on the ground.  It looks like it may have been a camp.

Mesquite wood here tonight for our cooking and heating.  We’re camped on a large beach at the intersections of these three canyons.  Kanab water is fairly good.  Slightly hard, but not in any way bitter.  Right now it flows around the smaller fist size rocks, and is only about 10ft. wide.  There is however ample evidence of past high water. 

 JRS

 Didn’t get far today.  Six miles down Hack-Kanab and here we camp at their confluence.  A beautiful day.  Too beautiful to grunt and sweat, and so we gave in to it.  Climbed out on the Esplanade and horsed around a bit in space country.  Aisles and alleys, streets and byways -- all sorts of nooks, but no corners.  A stone wilderness.  Sunny today again.  Took off my clothes and aired crotch and ass.  Bathed in Kanab Creek.  Cold enough to give you a headache. 

Found an old trail up to the Esplanade opposite Hack Canyon.  In great disrepair, but surprisingly there were shod hoof prints along it.  After dinner of jerky chili and lentils we discovered some pictographs in an overhang.  Just up from Hack Canyon. 

We’re doing real good even if we are just lazing along.  Still a day ahead of schedule.  It’s nice to be able to afford to just take it easy.  Take our time and see the country.  We have so much food too, both with us and cached that we probably couldn’t eat it all in twice the time.  So nice to just meander with the canyon, bask in the sun.  We’ll let our bodies get used to this pleasantly, gradually. 

Heard a series of drum rolls while walking in the Esplanade; deep booming tone that resonated the body cavity.  A pause pause, half-step hesitation, breath caught.  Indian drums?  Or explosives up Hack Canyon?  Other than that, this country is pure desert silence with only a small fraction of the air traffic in the heart of the canyon.  It’s such a rare and precious experience.  The canyon without planes.  Very easy to lose yourself.
