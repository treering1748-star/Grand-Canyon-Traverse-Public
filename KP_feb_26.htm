JRS

Dragon Spring. Or at least what used to be Dragon Spring, since it is now dry where once water ran. The main creek bed above the spring, which used to be dry, is now on the other hand running full over what was once a dry fall. Very pretty waterfall now, tucked back in a Tapeats slot. Walked upstream a ways to where the Tapeats starts, and the water still flowed strong. It appears that Dragon Spring has changed its source. Perhaps tomorrow we’ll find its new location.

We’re nearly out of food. Tonight we ate the last of our lentils, with the last of our chili, garlic, jerky, sausage, and onion flakes. We have decided to go for it all the way, and make Phantom Ranch tomorrow afternoon. The fear of hunger and the thought of beer are driving us on. I always figured that we could make Phantom from here in a day, and that night we had planned to stay in upper Phantom Canyon was always just an extra day if we needed it. Well we don’t need it, and we’re a little homesick, and ready for it to be over. It does make me some sad though to think this is really our last night in the wilderness. It has been, well, quite a walk and that’s a fact.

Was pretty quick going today walking upstream through Crystal’s granite gorge. Much easier and more pleasant than I remember it. More water in the creek this time and the stream has changed its course within the walls a couple of places. No difficulties at all, except for wet feet. I think my boots are gonna last long enough to get me outa here. Just barely. By the end of all of this, the Canyon will have eaten another pair of my boots.  May the gods have mercy on their soles.

 Feeling pretty good, all things considered. The hunger pangs in my stomach sated for now. They’re mostly in my head anyway, triggered by a light food bag.

 TWS

Overnight the river had increased its flow, so that as we rose this morning the sound of the rapids made us look, and then look again. Crystal Rapid is a mighty cauldron of boiling mud; all the water shoots through narrows off the end of the beach, perhaps 40ft. wide. The biggest waves rise 10ft. or so, then crash down; water behind rushes up, and then crashes again.

As the sun peeked over the rim to the east and light touched the beach, we hoisted our burdens and began up Crystal. It’s a granite gorge to rival Bright Angel, and with plenty of water, often wall to wall. Inside the first mile we had already crossed a dozen or more times and boots were filled. After awhile we gave up boulder hopping, and just slogged through. The canyon bottom is fairly open however, especially compared to what we’re used to (i.e., Kanab) and the first few morning hours of work got us here to Dragon Spring. It’s a couple of miles upstream from the junction of Dragon and Crystal. The Tapeats here form a narrow defile, winding around upon itself. Just up canyon a fine waterfall comes over a barrier. Our route tomorrow leads up a break between the falls and our camp.

So today and tonight are our last in the wilderness. Tomorrow, people, telephones, real food, beer -- Phantom Ranch. We are ready for them, eager to emerge from our self exile. Such a long walk.  There has been pain, misery, exhaustion, and there has been awe and amazement. We have placed ourselves in a position of almost total detachment from the world and accepted the possible perilous consequences. We came close. But we’ve made it through and now we are ready to return, boy are we ready -- to get back to our comfortable home, the ladies and the babies. The harsh reality of wilderness breeds appreciation for the comforts.
