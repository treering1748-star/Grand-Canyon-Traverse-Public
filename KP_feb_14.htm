 TWS

         Ten miles along the Colorado. It’s debatable whether today was any easier than yesterday. Once again we were boulder hopping, climbing up and over and around. The scenery was definitely a change however. The Colorado is it’s dirty brown color of this time of year and we passed a couple of respectable rapids – Kanab, Fishtail, Cranberry, Deer Creek.

Waterfalls appeared in places coming off the Redwall and Mauve formations. Prettiest was the one coming down the little canyon formed by the Sinyala Fault on the south side of the river.

We made Fishtail for lunch and were fairly pooped already -- five long miles. Ahead was a long Mauve bluff which Butchart says you detour around. We found a cairned trail leading up on the downriver side and grunted up the steep slope, up above the bluff.  The trail wound in and out of the washes cutting through the bluff and often took us out on narrow ledges we had to siddle around -- 100ft. above the river.  Down off the bluff and around one more corner and we could see the beginnings of the ancient Vishnu Schist and the granite narrows above Deer Creek.

We stumbled over the rocks at the base of the falls, our eyes glued to the magnificent ribbon of water shooting out of the slot in the rock.  Deer Creek cuts down through this tongue of Tapeats like a hot knife through butter. Right at the river it comes out of the rock about midway up the Tapeats formation and falls over a hundred feet to the river level.

Jim spotted a nice size trout skitter down the stream toward the Colorado, and the fishing fever struck. Out came the line, hooks, and bait.  Alas, they wouldn’t bite, but it was quite enjoyable to see Jim jump from one hole to the next, energized and with that look in his eye.  After ten hard miles too!

One more grunt climb on the trail leading up on the left brought us up above the falls and a trail leading along a Tapeats ledge.  Deer Creek winds 30 to 40ft. down into the slot and the sound of the rushing water fills the canyon to a roar.

We’re camped above the Tapeats under some cottonwoods where we can see up canyon to where Deer Spring is and where the left fork continues up. We’re both very tired, and rain this afternoon and tonight dampens our spirits. We look forward to rest day tomorrow.

 JRS

            Another hard day.  Boulder hopping along the river and traversing talus slopes, loose tricky rock.  The river is brown and every side canyon is flowing.  Saw two good seeps above the river, one at Deer Creek and another about a mile downstream. The geology of this stretch is intricate and beautiful. Saw one Precambrian fold a mile below Deer Creek and a definite dome shaped intrusion just where you start to climb over the bluff on the downhill side. There are nice outcrops of Vishnu Schist and Bass limestone, the latter of which forms boulders with an iridescent metallic blue sheen by the river. The slump formation which forms Surprise Valley and Deer Creek Valley has had some interesting effects.  It completely buries the Tapeats except for outcrops here in Deer Creek. The Tapeats here seems to form a ridge across the lower valley through which the stream has cut its famous threading channel and falls.

We are both very tired and the weather has not improved. We have been debating whether it is worth going for the cache as that would necessitate a whole day of pure grunt work. We are concerned with the need to conserve our energy. However while we’re still not low on food, it is problematic whether or not we have enough to take us safely to the next cache.  Certainly we’d be on short rations by then.  

Heavy rain most of the night, but the tarp is proving to be excellent shelter. Rest day tomorrow. Everything is very green here even though it’s winter. The cottonwoods are bare, of course, but the willow still have leaves and the grass is lush. There is a heavy riparian smell to the air. Tried some fishing just after we got to Deer Creek, but no luck even though you could see the trout. This place is beautiful and we have it all to ourselves, the weather precluding even aircraft. We are very alone and I am even a little scared. We have many long hard miles to go.
