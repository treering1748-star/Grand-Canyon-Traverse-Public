           TWS

           I arrived here Friday the 17th at 6:00 PM and met Jim and Deb at the Tusayan Bar. We had a couple of pitchers and headed out to the cabin. The road from the Village was pretty bad but traversable because it was around 8:00 PM and the mud and water was frozen. We made it as far as a mile from the cabin and we got stuck in arroyo with a steep hill to climb. The snow had drifted in there mainly because it was hidden from the sun and so it doesn’t melt off. We trudged in the moonlight on back to the cabin, feeling high in spirits, despite the obvious set back to our trip. We decided to return in the morning and hopefully get it un-stuck. To top things off, I remembered that I had forgotten to call Suzanne and tell her I had made the trip safely, and I had told her I would.  I didn’t sleep well that night knowing that she wasn’t either.The next morning (Saturday) found us trying to start the pickup and failing. The battery went dead. So we removed the battery to warm it in sunlight hoping that’ll give it more juice, and discovered that it is dry as a bone. “Somebody” forgot to fill it!

Thoroughly discouraged now we walk back to the cabin for lunch. After lunch we start back to the pickup. We fill the battery and hook it up and the truck starts immediately. We work the rest of the morning and manage to get the truck stuck even worse. Now we are discouraged. How are we going to get out to the trailhead?

Next day we decide to walk to the village - I must call Suzanne - she must have the police, Park Service, and the rest of the world looking for me! We get as far as the rock house and Chris gives us a ride to town – our luck is changing. I get a hold of Suzanne, and all is well.  We get extra food at Bobbitt's. We stop in at El Tovar Bar and have 3 apiece. On the way back Loren picks us up. He’s Supai Indian and very friendly. We smoke a joint, then return to the Village for gas, and then he gives us a ride almost to the rock house. No mean feat either as it is about 4:00 PM and the mud is at a height of the gooey-ness! From there we walk home and arrive about 6:00 PM.

After dinner we decide to walk to Bass -- about 26 miles, or two days. Maybe we’ll get picked up. If not, the walk will be good for us. We will leave tomorrow Tuesday, February 21. This morning we are making final preparations and we are in good spirits.
