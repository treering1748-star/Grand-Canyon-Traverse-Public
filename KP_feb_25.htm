JRS

Crystal Creek on the Colorado. Not a cloud all day. We walked the Tonto on the west side of Tuna and went down through the Tapeats at a break on the Mauve fault. It was a steep boulder strewn ravine that could have been dangerous. It felt that way. A fall on this razor edged schist or abrasive granite would be very painful.

The river is very high. The beach traverse from Tuna to Crystal is nonexistent. In all, we had to traverse over a mile, crabwise, clinging to the rock, scrambling and bouldering.

Found a 20hp Mercury outboard motor at the lower end of the beach. How and why it is there is a mystery. Tom wanted to cache and come back for it, but how the hell would you get it out? I beach combed this afternoon, and Tom fished. He came back in an hour or so with 3, and I tried it. Though I fished the rest of the afternoon, I got bites in only one pool. The fish in that pool was one lone monster that broke my line three times. Now all I have left is one hook. I went back to the pool several times and managed to get a rise out of him about three more times. But I could not land him.

This now connects the routes I know from before, and now we are in country I’ve walked before. Much of the uncertainty that has always been with us on this hike is gone. We’re nearly home. I’m ready to come out now myself, to take a shower, have a beer and a smoke. It has been good, oh, so very good a hike. Canyon!

TWS

Having little faith, and noticing high clouds covering the sky, we set up the tarp last night. It didn’t rain a drop, and this morning was crystal clear blue.

The Tonto above Tuna was open, for the most part; just a little gully here and there to slow us up. In no time at all we were looking down through the break in the Tapeats west of the mouth of Tuna. Once again, a fault ravine route. It was a sharp change to drop off the safe, predictable Tonto into this crack leading down through the rock. The Colorado ran fast, full and red down below. We picked our way down the ravine, climbing down facing the rock, and at times scooting down with our packs to the rock. Several up and over traverses were necessary to get past barrier falls. Very reminiscent of yesterday’s Redwall descent.

When we reached the Colorado it became obvious we would have to traverse around the corner, up river to the mouth of Tuna along the granite. Up and over, down around -- half the time balancing on river polished rock, the other climbing, packs on, over it.  Tuna ran only a small flow of water, hardly more than what went by our campsite up at the head of the Tapeats.

Another 3/4 mile of granite traverse and 1/2 mile of boulder hopping delivered us here -- Crystal Rapids. Beautiful spot. Caught 3 trout for supper. Dozed in the sun on the beach. Explored Indian ruins on terrace.
