Grand Canyon Traverse

 

These web pages are a record of two extraordinary hikes.  The two trips were made by my brother and I -- Jim and Tom Swetnam.  The first trip was a traverse below the South Rim of the Grand Canyon, from Bass Trailhead to Hualapai Hilltop, via Great Thumb Mesa, from February 20 to March 10, 1978.  The second was a traverse below the North Rim from Hack Canyon to Phantom Ranch, and then out at Yaki Point on the South Rim, from February 10 to 28, 1980.

In these hyperlinked pages you will find transcribed text from the journals that we kept during the trip.  (Jim’s entries are indicated by his initials JRS, and mine are TWS).  Each web page is a single day of journal entries (with one exception), and in the left column of each page you will see thumbnail images of maps and photographs relevant to that day.  

So, you may be asking:  Why bother to develop web pages to document a couple of hikes that were completed more than 25 years ago?  I’ve tried to capture some of the reasons why these trips are important to me in an essay that is linked here.  Beyond these personal reasons, I felt a need to secure the journals and photographs for the future in a digital form.  In this form, I can easily make multiple copies and distribute them in multiple places.  I also wanted to share this stuff with Jim (because I have possession of the journals and photos), and with family and friends who may find some parts interesting, if not amusing.  Last, it has been a fun project to do in my spare time  – an arm-chair adventure in my middle age, re-living some very fine days of my youth! 

Tom Swetnam, March 2005
