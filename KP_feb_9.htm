TWS

Jim and I left San Lorenzo Wednesday for Tucson.  Spent the night in the Coronado National Forest just outside Saguaro National Monument. We camped beneath Saguaros on the lower hills of the Santa Catalina Mountains, overlooking the lights of Tucson. 

I talked to the professors at UA and tree-ring lab and we picked up a couple of topo maps of the Hack-Kanab area – our destination tomorrow. 

Now we are camped in the Ponderosa’s outside Tusayan -  anxious in anticipation for the start of our trip. We arrived here Thursday night from Tucson, and spent all day Friday hunting down people, looking for someone to drive the V. W. back from the North Rim.   No luck. Everyone either down in the canyon, or just not at home.  Our luck changed this morning, and we found Larry Powell at home and willing to drive the bug home for us.  Jim and I put new points in her and set the timing.  Hopefully we won’t encounter any difficulties with the bug on the way over, and Larry won’t on the way back. 

We visited the back country desk in the VC yesterday and laid out our trip and plans.  No trouble there.  They just listened carefully and asked a few questions concerning the route and wrote out our permit for eighteen days -- February 11 to February 29.  The ranger mentioned that a party taking a very similar trip – Kanab to Phantom – had been flown out of Mauve Saddle with the beginnings of frostbite last month.   Evidently they had got caught in bad weather and had to hole up there in the cabin for three days.  $292.00 helicopter cost to fly them out.  Jim and I looked at each other and I’m sure both reached the same conclusion that this alternative was most undesirable.

We hope for good weather, but know that we will face bad sooner or later.  We believe our determination and preparedness for the worst will overcome bad or severe conditions.  Tomorrow the trailhead.
