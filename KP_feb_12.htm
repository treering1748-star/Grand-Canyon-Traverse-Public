JRS

 A very good day today. The first day we really stretched our muscles. We are 10-12 miles from the river deep in the Redwall of Kanab at the junction of an unnamed side canyon. We are camped on a triangular island of grass and mesquite, the delta of the side canyon, and a power spot. We sat and munched venison jerky when we got here, looking up at the sky, which is a blue Y; the intersection of mighty walls.  

Downstream the grinding action of enormous boulders has carved an overhanging amphitheater, red streaked with desert varnish.  To stand at the focus is to hear the river apparently flowing in the rock itself!  Here are talismans. A wizard’s wand of driftwood on a ledge, gypsum flowers growing on the overhanging Redwall, and calcite parallelepipeds.

We walked up the side canyon, a wonderland boulder hopping over deep air clear pools of the best water we’ve found yet. There are fingerling trout in Kanab this far up. We began seeing them below a spring, a half mile up creek. This is a good sign.  We feel that the creek is constant from here on. There was about a two mile stretch where it disappeared below Jump Up.  No doubt due to the heavy gravel deposit.

It’s warm here tonight. The walls perhaps prevent much heat radiation to outer space. The air remains perfectly transparent, yet our vision has narrowed with the walls. This meandering travel deadens the senses sometimes; always peering around the next corner, hoping the next destination is there. The body is settling in now, the turds firming up (I hope). The bouldering session up the side canyon loosened my bones and scattered my brains. The canyon is beginning to get through to me!  Ah! The Redwall!

 TWS

 First full day of walking. We spent our morning energy moving down canyon with the first stop at Chamberlain Canyon and the first appearance of Redwall. We decided lunch would be at Jump Up which we figured couldn’t be more than three miles. It turned out to be a long three miles; each corner we turned we kept expecting to see the slash on the left, and boulders strewn, indicative of a major side canyon.  Down through the Redwall.  Finally Jump Up appeared - no more than 40ft. wide wall to wall, and with 300ft. of vertical height on either side. Just a corridor cutting through the rock.

Lunch and a long hours rest in the sunshine and we were up for another push down canyon. Three miles downstream from Jump Up and an hours walk and we’re camped here at the mouth of a large unnamed side canyon. With our packs unloaded and our backs unburdened our bodies and our spirits are lightened.  Our eyes clear from the heavy exertion and the fantastic beauty of this spot nearly overwhelms us.

Up Kanab and down are 400ft. Redwall cliffs! Amphitheaters and huge smoothly overhanging walls.  Chutes carved by flood and boulder. One wall is very reminiscent of White House Ruin in Canyon de Chelly – long streaks of desert varnish pointing down to a ledge.

We walked to a spot just beneath and in front of the ledge and the creek sound behind us was reflected perfectly, so that all sound seemed to come directly from the rock!  A most interesting phenomena.

Later we boulder climbed up the side canyon and found good pure water in huge Redwall tanks. Clear as a bell, 3 and 4ft. to their bottoms.   Redbud and cottonwoods grow in among the boulders and live from the water.

Slowly we are working down to the river.  The Rio Colorado.  We are working for it though, as our packs are heavy and the river bottom is rocky and it winds around and around.  One more day of this Redwall alley and will be on the bottom – bedrock  Precambrian.
